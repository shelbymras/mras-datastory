My Data Story

Personal Data Section

When I first thought about my digital practices the first thing that came to mind was how much I get distracted by notifications during my daily life. Then I started thinking about how much I interact with those notification specifically while I'm doing school work. That's when I started out on a five day long data collection adventure, logging how many times I interacted with notifications while doing school work.

At the end of my collection I had logged data on what app notification distracted me, while doing work for one of my five class, and for how long it distracted me (among other things). I found that text messages were my primary form of distraction, and I mostly get distracted while doing work for my writing intensive classes.

Social Data Section

After I collected data on my personal digital practices, I was then challenged to see how my practices are part of something bigger. Thats when I learned about data brokering. The video below gives an overview of what data brokering essentially is, and why the practice of data brokering is potentially invasive to the average internet user.

Through the creation of this video I learned about the information that is constantly being collected about me, and millions of others without our knowledge. Though various government organizations like the FTC and FCC have called for more transparency in data broker practices, there is still an overwhelming amount of information being collected daily about internet-users.
